How this ROI calculator works

Return on investment compares the money you got back to the money you put in. The headline figure is your total return:

ROI = (final value − amount invested) / amount invested × 100

So if you invested $10,000 and it's now worth $16,000, your net gain is $6,000 and your total return is 60%. That single number is great for comparing one-off results, but it says nothing about how long the money was working.

Total return vs. annualized return (CAGR)

To compare investments that ran for different lengths of time, you need the annualized return, also called the compound annual growth rate (CAGR). It is the steady yearly rate that would grow your starting amount up to the final value:

CAGR = (final value / amount invested)(1 / years) − 1

That same 60% total return looks very different depending on the holding period: over 1 year it's a 60% annual return, but spread over 5 years it's only about 9.9% per year. Enter a holding period above and the calculator fills in the annualized figure automatically; leave it blank and it simply shows the total return.

Getting an accurate ROI

  • Count every dollar in. Add purchase commissions, fees, or improvement costs to the amount invested so your return isn't flattered.
  • Use net proceeds out. For a sale, the final value should be what you actually received after selling costs.
  • Returns are nominal. This tool doesn't adjust for tax or inflation — a 9.9% return during 4% inflation is closer to 5.7% in real, spending-power terms.
  • CAGR hides the bumps. A smooth annualized rate doesn't mean the investment grew evenly each year — it can mask large swings along the way.

Frequently asked questions

How do you calculate return on investment (ROI)?

Total return is (final value − amount invested) ÷ amount invested, expressed as a percent. For example, turning $10,000 into $16,000 is a $6,000 gain, or a 60% total return.

What is the difference between total return and annualized return (CAGR)?

Total return is the whole gain over the entire holding period, ignoring how long it took. Annualized return, or CAGR, is the constant yearly rate that would compound your starting amount up to the final value: CAGR = (final ÷ initial)(1 ÷ years) − 1. A 60% total return over 5 years is only about 9.9% per year.

Does this ROI calculator include fees, taxes, or inflation?

No. It compares the cash you put in to the value you have now, so any fees or commissions you paid are only reflected if you fold them into the amount invested or net them out of the final value. Returns are nominal — they are not adjusted for tax or inflation.
