About MoneyMath

Clear, free finance calculators that give you a straight answer — without the sign-up walls, pop-ups, or spreadsheets.

Why we built this

Most people facing a money decision — a mortgage, a car loan, a savings goal — just want one honest number: what will this actually cost, or be worth? Too many calculators bury that answer under email gates, advertising mazes, and confusing inputs. MoneyMath exists to do the opposite: type a few figures, get a clear result instantly, and move on with your day.

How it works

Every calculator on this site runs the same way. When you enter your numbers, the math happens right here on your device using a single, shared calculation engine. That engine is unit-tested, so the formula behind the mortgage calculator is the identical, audited code used by the loan, auto loan, compound interest, retirement, and debt payoff tools. One core, consistently applied, means you can trust the answer regardless of which page you land on.

Free, and private by design

MoneyMath is free to use. We keep the lights on with unobtrusive advertising, which is why you'll see the occasional ad slot — not paywalls. Crucially, the figures you enter never leave your browser. We don't have a database of your salary or your loan balance because the calculations never travel to a server in the first place. You can read exactly how we handle cookies and advertising on our privacy page.

An honest disclaimer

We are calculator builders, not your financial adviser. The tools here are designed to be accurate and genuinely useful for planning, but they produce estimates based on the inputs you provide. For decisions that matter, confirm the figures with your lender, bank, or a licensed professional. See our full disclaimer for details.
