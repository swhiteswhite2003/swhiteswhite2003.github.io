How this savings goal calculator works

This savings goal calculator answers one question: how much do I need to save per month to reach a target by a set date? Instead of guessing, it solves the future-value-of-an-annuity formula backwards for the deposit:

PMT = (FV − P(1 + i)n) · i / ((1 + i)n − 1)

where FV is your savings goal, P is the amount already saved, i is the monthly return (your annual rate ÷ 12), and n is the number of months (years × 12). Your starting balance and every deposit compound each month, so the calculator finds the level monthly amount that grows everything you have to exactly your target on the deadline.

Why compound growth shrinks the amount you need

The bigger your expected return and the longer your timeline, the more the market does the heavy lifting — and the less you have to deposit yourself. The Projected growth (interest) figure in the result is the gap between your goal and what you actually contribute. On a long horizon that gap can easily be a third or more of the goal, which is exactly why starting early matters so much.

Three ways to lower your required monthly saving

  • Give yourself more time. Adding a few years spreads the goal over more deposits and lets returns compound longer, dropping the monthly figure sharply.
  • Seed it with a lump sum. Anything you enter under "already saved" grows on its own — raising it cuts the monthly amount more than you'd expect.
  • Earn a higher return. Moving a long-term goal from a 0% cash account to an invested return reduces what you personally need to set aside (at the cost of more risk).

Set a realistic return

For a short-term, can't-lose-it goal — a house deposit in two years, an emergency fund — use 0% and count only your own deposits. For a long-term, invested goal, a conservative figure keeps the plan honest: assume too high a return and you'll under-save and miss the target. When in doubt, lower the rate and let any extra growth be a pleasant surprise.

Frequently asked questions

How much should I save each month to reach my goal?

Enter your target amount, what you've already saved, how many years you have, and the annual return you expect. The calculator solves for the level monthly deposit that — combined with compound growth — grows your starting balance to exactly your target by the end date.

Does this account for investment growth and interest?

Yes. Both your starting balance and every monthly deposit earn the expected annual return, compounded monthly. That's why the total you contribute is usually less than your goal — the projected growth line shows how much the returns add on top.

What return rate should I assume?

Use 0% for a plain savings account or cash goal so you only count your own deposits. For a diversified long-term investment, many people model 4–7% as a conservative real-world estimate. Lower the rate to be cautious — a smaller assumed return means a larger required monthly deposit.

What happens if I'm already on track?

If your current savings, growing at the return you entered, are projected to reach the goal on their own, the required monthly contribution comes back at $0 and the calculator confirms you're already on track — no new deposits needed.

Estimates are for general information only and are not financial advice. Projections assume a constant return that real savings and investments will not match exactly. Confirm your plan with a qualified financial professional.
