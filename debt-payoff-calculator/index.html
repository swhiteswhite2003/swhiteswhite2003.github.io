How this debt payoff calculator works

This debt payoff calculator — also used as a debt snowball calculator — models paying down several debts at once. Each month it charges interest on every balance, pays the minimum on each, then throws every spare dollar at one focus debt until it disappears. The freed-up payment then rolls onto the next debt. That rollover is exactly why payoff speeds up the longer you stick with it.

Monthly interest on each balance is simply:

monthly interest = balance × (APR ÷ 12)

Your total monthly commitment stays the same every month — the sum of all your minimum payments plus the extra amount you enter — so as each debt clears, more money piles onto the next one.

Snowball vs avalanche: which should you pick?

  • Debt snowball — orders your debts smallest balance first. You clear a whole debt quickly, get a motivating win, and build momentum. It usually costs a little more interest.
  • Debt avalanche — orders your debts highest APR first. By killing your most expensive interest soonest it pays the least total interest and often finishes a touch sooner.

The calculator shows you both: switch the method and watch the total interest change. For most people the dollar difference is modest, so the best method is the one you will actually keep going with.

Three ways to be debt-free faster

  • Add an extra payment. Even $50 a month compounds, because it rolls forward to every debt after the first one clears.
  • Never shrink your payment. As balances fall, keep paying the same total every month — that constant payment is what powers the snowball.
  • Avoid taking on new debt while you pay down. A fresh balance resets your momentum and adds new interest.

Frequently asked questions

What is the debt snowball method?

Pay the minimum on every debt, then put every spare dollar toward your smallest balance first. When that debt is gone, roll its whole payment onto the next-smallest balance. The quick early wins keep you motivated.

What is the debt avalanche method?

It uses the same rollover idea, but you attack the debt with the highest APR first. Because you kill your most expensive interest soonest, the avalanche minimises the total interest you pay and is the cheapest route to debt-free.

Which is better, snowball or avalanche?

Avalanche always costs the same or less in interest, while snowball delivers faster psychological wins. This calculator shows both totals so you can weigh motivation against the dollars saved — the best method is the one you will actually stick with.

How much faster do extra payments make me debt-free?

Every extra dollar goes straight to your focus debt's principal, and once that debt clears the payment rolls onto the next one, so the effect snowballs. Enter an extra monthly amount above to see your new debt-free date and the interest you save.
