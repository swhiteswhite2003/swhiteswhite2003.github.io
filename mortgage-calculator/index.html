How this mortgage calculator works

Your monthly principal and interest (P&I) is found with the standard amortizing-loan formula:

M = P · r(1 + r)n / ((1 + r)n − 1)

where P is the loan amount (home price minus down payment), r is the monthly interest rate (your APR ÷ 12), and n is the number of monthly payments (loan term in years × 12). Property tax and home insurance, if entered, are divided by 12 and added on top to estimate your full monthly housing cost — often called PITI.

Why your interest rate matters so much

On a 30-year loan you make 360 payments, so even a small rate difference compounds into tens of thousands of dollars. Try nudging the rate up or down by half a point and watch the total interest move — that is the single biggest lever on what your home really costs.

Three ways to pay less interest

  • Bigger down payment. Less borrowed means less interest and a smaller payment.
  • Shorter term. A 15-year loan has a higher monthly payment but far less total interest than a 30-year.
  • Extra monthly payments. Any extra goes straight to principal — enter an amount above to see how many years it shaves off.

Frequently asked questions

How is my monthly mortgage payment calculated?

The principal-and-interest portion uses M = P · r(1+r)n / ((1+r)n − 1). We then add any monthly property tax and insurance you enter.

Does this include property tax and insurance?

Yes — both are optional fields. Leave them at 0 for principal and interest only, or enter your annual amounts to see your full estimated monthly cost (PITI).

How much do extra payments save?

Extra payments reduce principal directly, so they cut both your payoff time and total interest. The result box updates the payoff time as soon as you enter an extra amount.
