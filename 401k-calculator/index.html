How this 401(k) calculator works

Each month you set aside a slice of your salary, and many employers add a match on top. This 401k calculator turns those inputs into a single monthly contribution and grows it with monthly compounding until your retirement age.

Your own contribution is salary × your % ÷ 12. The employer match is salary × min(your %, match limit) ÷ 12 × match rate — the employer only matches up to its limit, so contributing 10% when the limit is 6% still only earns a match on the first 6%. Both streams are added together and compounded:

FV = P(1 + i)N + PMT · ((1 + i)N − 1) / i

where P is your current balance, PMT is the combined monthly contribution, i is your expected annual return ÷ 12, and N is the number of months until retirement (years × 12). Investment growth is simply the projected balance minus everything that was actually paid in.

Why the employer match is free money

A 50% match up to 6% of pay means that for every dollar you contribute (up to 6% of your salary) your employer hands you another 50 cents — an instant 50% return before the market does anything. If your contribution rate is below the match limit, you are leaving guaranteed money on the table. The fastest, lowest-risk improvement most people can make is to bump their contribution up to at least the full match.

Three ways to grow your 401(k) faster

  • Capture the full match first. Contribute at least up to the match limit before doing anything else — it is the highest-return dollar in your plan.
  • Start early and let time work. Because growth compounds, the first dollars you invest do the heaviest lifting. Try moving your current age down a few years to see the effect.
  • Raise your rate gradually. Even a one- or two-point bump in your contribution percentage can add a large sum over decades — adjust the field above and watch the projected balance respond.

Frequently asked questions

How does 401(k) employer matching work?

Many employers match part of what you put in — for example, 50% of your contributions up to 6% of your pay. This calculator multiplies your salary by the lower of your contribution rate and the match limit, then by the match rate, to find the employer's monthly contribution. It is added to your own savings before growth is applied.

What is a good 401(k) contribution percentage?

A common starting point is to contribute at least enough to capture the full employer match — leaving it on the table is turning down free money. Many savers then aim for 10% to 15% of pay including the match. Raise your rate above the match limit field and watch the projected balance climb.

How much will my 401(k) be worth at retirement?

It depends on your starting balance, how much you and your employer add each month, your expected annual return, and how many years are left until retirement. Enter those values and the calculator compounds them monthly using FV = P(1+i)N + PMT·((1+i)N − 1)/i to project your balance.

Does this 401(k) calculator account for taxes and inflation?

No. To keep the projection simple it ignores salary raises, inflation, investment fees, and the taxes you will eventually owe on traditional 401(k) withdrawals. The result is a pre-tax balance assuming a steady contribution and return. Note that the IRS also caps how much you can defer each year, so very high contribution rates may not be allowed in practice — check the current year's limit.
