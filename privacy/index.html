Privacy Policy

Last updated: . This page explains what data MoneyMath does and does not collect, and the role cookies and advertising play.

The short version

The numbers you type into our calculators — loan amounts, balances, salary, interest rates — are processed entirely in your browser. They are never transmitted to us, never stored on a server, and never sold. We use cookies only for advertising and basic, anonymous analytics, both of which you can control or turn off. That's it.

Calculator inputs stay on your device

Every calculation on MoneyMath runs as JavaScript inside your own browser. When you enter figures into the mortgage, loan, auto loan, compound interest, retirement, or debt payoff tools, that data lives only in the page you're viewing. We have no database of your inputs because they are never sent to one. Close or refresh the tab and they're gone.

Cookies we use

A cookie is a small text file a website stores in your browser. MoneyMath uses cookies for the following purposes:

  • Cookie consent. When you dismiss the cookie banner, we save a single flag in your browser's local storage (mm_cookie_ok) so the banner doesn't reappear on every visit. This never leaves your device.
  • Advertising. We use Google AdSense to display ads. Google and its partners may set cookies to serve and measure ads, including personalised ads where permitted.
  • Analytics. Where enabled, we use Google Analytics to understand, in aggregate, which pages are popular and how the site performs. This uses cookies to distinguish visitors anonymously.

Google AdSense & advertising cookies

MoneyMath is supported by advertising served through Google AdSense. As a result:

  • Third-party vendors, including Google, use cookies to serve ads based on your prior visits to this and other websites.
  • Google's use of advertising cookies enables it and its partners to serve ads to you based on your visits to MoneyMath and/or other sites on the internet.
  • You can opt out of personalised advertising by visiting Google Ads Settings. You can also opt out of many third-party vendors' use of cookies for personalised advertising at aboutads.info/choices or youronlinechoices.eu.

For full details, see How Google uses cookies in advertising and the Google Privacy Policy.

Analytics

If analytics is active, Google Analytics collects standard, anonymised information such as the pages you view, your approximate region, and your device and browser type. We use this only to improve the site. We do not use it to identify you personally, and we never combine it with anything you type into a calculator. Learn more in the Google Privacy Policy.

How to control or remove cookies

You're in charge of cookies in your browser. You can:

  • Block or delete cookies through your browser settings (Chrome, Safari, Firefox, and Edge all let you do this).
  • Use your browser's private/incognito mode to limit persistent cookies.
  • Opt out of personalised ads using the links in the AdSense section above.

Blocking cookies won't stop the calculators from working — they run regardless — though some ads may become less relevant.

Children's privacy

MoneyMath is a general-audience information site and is not directed at children under 13. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children.

Changes to this policy

We may update this policy as the site evolves or as legal requirements change. When we do, we'll revise the "last updated" date at the top of this page.
